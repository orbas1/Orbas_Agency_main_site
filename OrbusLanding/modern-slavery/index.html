Transparency & Ethics

Modern Slavery and Human Trafficking Statement

This statement is made by Blackridge Group Ltd, trading as Orbas, for the financial year ending 31 March 2025.

Our Business and Structure

Blackridge Group Ltd operates the Orbas ecosystem comprising Orbas AI, Orbas Freelance, Orbas Learn and Orbas Services. We are an early-stage company with lean teams supporting customers in the UK and beyond.

Our supply chain is limited but expanding, and we are committed to preventing modern slavery, servitude, forced or compulsory labour, and human trafficking in every relationship we form.

Policies & Governance

  • A supplier code of conduct and contract clauses that set clear expectations on labour practices.
  • Procurement checklists that record ownership, labour policies and audit history for new partners.
  • Escalation routes through the Ethics Office with board visibility on any material concerns.
  • Alignment with our ESG and Equality commitments.

Risk Assessment

  • Mapping suppliers by category and geography to identify higher-risk services.
  • Requesting evidence of fair recruitment and worker protections from labour providers supporting Orbas Freelance and managed services.
  • Reviewing datacentre, content production and hardware partners against international labour standards.

Due Diligence

We conduct proportionate due diligence on suppliers, partners and contractors before onboarding and through periodic reviews. Activities include:

  • Checking compliance with the Modern Slavery Act 2015, labour laws and relevant human rights standards.
  • Including contractual commitments to ethical labour practices and audit rights.
  • Requesting policies, certifications or references that evidence safe working conditions.
  • Working with suppliers on remediation plans where risks or gaps are identified.

Training & Awareness

Employees involved in procurement, people operations, legal and delivery receive annual briefings on modern slavery indicators and escalation steps.

We encourage anyone connected to Orbas to report concerns via ethics@orbas.io. Reports are acknowledged quickly and handled confidentially.

Effectiveness & Next Steps

  • Tracking supplier compliance through annual attestations and recorded follow-up actions.
  • Adding modern slavery indicators to the Impact centre once data collection is mature.
  • Partnering with industry groups to share best practices and strengthen monitoring.
  • Reviewing this statement every year and updating it as our operations grow.

This statement was approved by the Board of Directors of Blackridge Group Ltd on 30 June 2025.
