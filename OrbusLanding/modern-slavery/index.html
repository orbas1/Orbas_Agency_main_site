Transparency & Ethics

Modern Slavery and Human Trafficking Statement

This statement is made by Blackridge Group Ltd, trading as Orbas, for the financial year ending 31 March 2025.

Our Business and Structure

Blackridge Group Ltd operates the Orbas ecosystem comprising Orbas AI, Orbas Freelance, Orbas Learn and Orbas Services. We provide technology solutions, digital talent, learning content and managed services to enterprises globally.

We have a modest direct workforce and collaborate with a wider network of suppliers, contractors and partners. Our commitment is to ensure that no form of modern slavery, servitude, forced or compulsory labour, or human trafficking occurs in any part of our business or supply chains.

Policies & Governance

  • Supplier Code of Conduct outlining zero tolerance for forced labour and trafficking.
  • Due diligence checklists embedded in procurement for technology, data and talent providers.
  • Escalation routes through our Ethics Office and board oversight via the Audit & Risk Committee.
  • Integration with our ESG and Equality commitments.

Risk Assessment

  • Annual mapping of supply chains, prioritising regions or categories with higher prevalence of modern slavery risks.
  • Enhanced vetting for labour providers supporting Orbas Freelance and managed service delivery.
  • Review of datacentre, content production and hardware suppliers for adherence to international labour standards.

Due Diligence

We conduct proportionate due diligence on suppliers, partners and contractors before onboarding and through periodic reviews. Activities include:

  • Assessing compliance with the Modern Slavery Act 2015, labour laws and international human rights frameworks.
  • Requiring contractual commitments to ethical labour practices and the right to audit.
  • Collecting documentation such as policies, certifications and third-party audit reports.
  • Providing support and remediation plans where risks or gaps are identified.

Training & Awareness

Employees in procurement, people operations, legal and delivery teams receive annual training on modern slavery indicators and escalation procedures. Education is extended to partners and community members through Orbas Learn resources.

We encourage anyone connected to Orbas to report concerns via ethics@orbas.io. Reports are investigated promptly and confidentially.

Effectiveness & Next Steps

  • Tracking supplier compliance through annual attestations and audits.
  • Embedding modern slavery risk indicators into our Impact reporting.
  • Collaborating with industry partners and NGOs to strengthen monitoring and remediation.
  • Reviewing this statement annually and updating it as our operations evolve.

This statement was approved by the Board of Directors of Blackridge Group Ltd on 30 June 2025.
