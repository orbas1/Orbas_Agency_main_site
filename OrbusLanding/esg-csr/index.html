Environmental, Social & Governance (ESG) and Corporate Social Responsibility

Our commitment to responsible growth evolves with the expanding computing demand behind Orbas AI, Orbas Freelance, Orbas Learn and Orbas Services.

Starting from Transparency

We are in the early stages of formalising an ESG and CSR programme for the Orbas ecosystem. Today we openly acknowledge that the rapid adoption of our AI, automation and talent platforms increases data processing volumes, server workloads and electricity consumption. While we do not yet claim carbon neutrality, Blackridge Group Ltd is building the governance to measure our footprint, reduce avoidable emissions and balance innovation with stewardship.

This statement is the first milestone in a roadmap that will deliver auditable reporting, supplier standards and measurable improvements across the environmental, social and governance pillars that matter to our customers, partners and communities.

Environmental Stewardship

  • Infrastructure Baseline: Mapping the energy profile of the compute clusters that power Orbas AI and our automation services.
  • Efficiency First: Designing workloads, code and data pipelines to minimise unnecessary processing cycles and scheduling batch tasks for off-peak grid times.
  • Renewable Alignment: Negotiating hosting agreements that prioritise datacentres with renewable energy certificates and transparent carbon reporting.
  • Lifecycle Thinking: Extending hardware use, refurbishing equipment and partnering with certified recycling providers.

Social Responsibility

  • People Experience: Embedding the culture and inclusion standards described in our Company Culture and Equality & Diversity statements.
  • Ethical Product Design: Applying human oversight to AI use cases, data sourcing and model deployment across Orbas AI and Orbas Learn.
  • Fair Gig Economy: Safeguarding creators and specialists on Orbas Freelance with transparent contracts, prompt payments and support.
  • Community Value: Expanding partnerships that make digital skills training and automation accessible to underserved groups.

Governance & Accountability

  • Board Oversight: The Blackridge Group Ltd board reviews ESG risks quarterly and assigns executive ownership for each pillar.
  • Policy Integration: Aligning our Modern Slavery Statement, privacy policy and compliance controls into a unified management system.
  • Stakeholder Dialogue: Engaging customers, suppliers and investors through surveys and briefings to inform our priorities.
  • Transparent Reporting: Preparing to publish annual ESG scorecards with progress metrics, starting FY2026.

2025 & 2026 ESG Roadmap

Our immediate focus is on measurement, setting achievable reduction targets and integrating ESG criteria into procurement and product decisions. Key milestones include:

  • Q3 2025: Complete greenhouse gas baseline and energy audit of our primary hosting partners.
  • Q4 2025: Launch ESG supplier code and begin onboarding datacentres with renewable commitments.
  • Q1 2026: Publish the first Orbas ESG dashboard within the Our Impact centre, integrating data from all four platforms.
  • Q2 2026: Release annual ESG report aligned to recognised frameworks such as SASB and TCFD.
